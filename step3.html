A Deep Dive into Step Three of Narcotics Anonymous

Step Three: "We made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him."

Insights from the Basic Text

The Basic Text presents Step Three as a pivotal point in recovery: moving from self-will to trusting a Power greater than ourselves.

  • Decision and Action: Step Three is not just about belief, but about making a conscious decision to trust and act differently.
  • Letting Go of Self-Will: Addicts often try to control everything. Step Three is about letting go of that control and accepting help.
  • "God as We Understood Him": The step is inclusiveâ€”members are encouraged to define their own concept of a Higher Power.
  • Freedom from Fear: Turning our will and life over brings relief from the fear and anxiety of trying to manage everything alone.
  • Foundation for Spiritual Growth: This step is the beginning of a new relationship with a Higher Power, which continues to develop through the steps.

The Basic Text emphasizes that Step Three is a process, often requiring repeated effort and growing trust.

Insights from "It Works: How and Why"

"It Works: How and Why" explores the spiritual and practical implications of Step Three.

  • Willingness and Faith: Making the decision to turn our will and lives over requires willingness and the beginnings of faith.
  • Trusting the Process: Step Three is about learning to trust the recovery process, the fellowship, and a Power greater than ourselves.
  • Letting Go of Outcomes: We stop trying to control results and start focusing on doing the next right thing.
  • Personal Understanding: Each member's understanding of a Higher Power is respected and encouraged to evolve.
  • Action Follows Decision: The decision of Step Three is put into action by working the rest of the steps and living the principles.

"It Works" highlights that Step Three is about surrender, trust, and beginning to live a new way.

Insights from the Step Working Guides

The Step Working Guides provide practical questions and exercises for Step Three.

  • What Does "Turning Over" Mean?: Encourages reflection on what it means to turn our will and lives over, and what that looks like in daily life.
  • Barriers to Surrender: Prompts members to identify fears, doubts, or reservations about letting go of control.
  • Developing Trust: Asks about experiences of trusting others, the fellowship, or a Higher Power, and how trust can grow.
  • Acting on Faith: Encourages taking small steps in faith, even when uncertain, and noticing the results.
  • Personal Experience: Invites members to share times when turning things over led to positive outcomes or relief.

The guides help make Step Three an ongoing, practical commitmentâ€”one that deepens with experience.

Key Themes & Synthesis of Step Three

Step Three is the step of decision, surrender, and trust. Key themes include:

1. Surrender and Trust

Step Three is about surrendering self-will and learning to trust a Power greater than ourselves, the NA program, and the process of recovery.

2. Action and Decision

Making a decision is only the beginningâ€”real change comes from putting that decision into action by working the steps and living the principles.

3. Freedom from Fear

Letting go of control brings relief from fear, anxiety, and the burden of trying to manage everything alone.

4. Personal Spiritual Path

Each member's relationship with a Higher Power is unique and respected. Step Three encourages exploration and growth.

5. Letting Go of Outcomes

Step Three is about focusing on doing the next right thing, rather than trying to control results.

6. Ongoing Process

Turning our will and lives over is not a one-time event, but an ongoing process that deepens as we continue in recovery.

Step Three is the turning point where we begin to live by spiritual principles, trusting that we are cared for and guided in our recovery.
