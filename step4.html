A Deep Dive into Step Four of Narcotics Anonymous

Step Four: "We made a searching and fearless moral inventory of ourselves."

Insights from the Basic Text

Step Four is the beginning of a new level of honesty and self-awareness. The Basic Text describes it as a courageous look at ourselves, both the good and the bad.

  • Searching: We look deeply into our behavior, motives, and patterns—past and present.
  • Fearless: We try to set aside fear and face the truth about ourselves, even if it’s uncomfortable.
  • Moral Inventory: This is not just a list of faults, but a balanced look at assets and liabilities.
  • Foundation for Change: By seeing ourselves honestly, we lay the groundwork for real growth and healing.
  • Letting Go of the Past: Inventory helps us let go of guilt, shame, and secrets that keep us sick.

The Basic Text encourages us to be thorough, honest, and compassionate with ourselves as we write our inventory.

Insights from "It Works: How and Why"

"It Works: How and Why" offers practical guidance and spiritual perspective on Step Four.

  • Purpose of Inventory: The inventory helps us identify patterns, resentments, fears, and harms done to others and ourselves.
  • Balance: We look at strengths as well as weaknesses, progress as well as problems.
  • Courage and Faith: Writing inventory takes courage and faith that the process will lead to healing, not condemnation.
  • Writing is Key: Putting our thoughts on paper brings clarity and helps break the cycle of denial and confusion.
  • Ongoing Process: Inventory is not a one-time event, but a tool we can use throughout recovery.

"It Works" reminds us that inventory is a loving act of self-care and the gateway to freedom from the past.

Insights from the Step Working Guides

The Step Working Guides provide questions and exercises for a thorough and personal Step Four.

  • Patterns and Triggers: Explore recurring behaviors, emotional triggers, and how they affect your life and relationships.
  • Resentments and Fears: List people, institutions, or situations you resent or fear, and examine the roots of these feelings.
  • Assets and Liabilities: Identify positive qualities and areas for growth, not just shortcomings.
  • Secrets and Shame: Inventory is a safe place to bring secrets and shame into the light, where they lose their power.
  • Self-Compassion: The guides encourage gentleness and honesty, not self-punishment.

The guides help make Step Four a structured, manageable, and healing process.

Key Themes & Synthesis of Step Four

Step Four is the step of courage, self-discovery, and the beginning of real change. Key themes include:

1. Honesty and Courage

Step Four asks us to be honest and courageous as we look at ourselves—our past, our patterns, our pain, and our progress.

2. Letting Go of Shame

Inventory helps us face and release the shame, guilt, and secrets that keep us stuck in addiction.

3. Self-Acceptance

We learn to accept ourselves as we are, with strengths and weaknesses, and become willing to change.

4. Healing and Growth

Step Four is the foundation for healing old wounds and building a new way of life.

5. Ongoing Process

Inventory is a tool we can return to whenever we need clarity, healing, or a fresh start.

6. Spiritual Progress

By facing ourselves honestly, we open the door to spiritual progress and deeper recovery.

Step Four is the courageous journey inward that leads to freedom, healing, and lasting change.
