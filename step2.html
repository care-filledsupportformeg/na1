A Deep Dive into Step Two of Narcotics Anonymous

Step Two: "We came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity."

Insights from the Basic Text

The Basic Text introduces Step Two as the beginning of hope and the possibility of change.

  • Restoration to Sanity: "Sanity" is defined as soundness of mind. For addicts, it means freedom from self-destructive thinking and behavior. Step Two is about believing that change is possible.
  • A Power Greater Than Ourselves: This step doesn't require defining or understanding a Higher Power, only the willingness to believe that help is possible from outside ourselves.
  • Open-mindedness: Step Two asks us to let go of past prejudices and to become open to new ideas and possibilities, especially regarding spirituality and recovery.
  • Hope: The step is often the first time many addicts feel genuine hope that they can recover and live differently.
  • The Importance of Fellowship: Seeing others recover helps members believe that recovery is possible for them, too.

The Basic Text emphasizes that Step Two is a process, not a single event. Willingness and open-mindedness are key.

Insights from "It Works: How and Why"

"It Works: How and Why" explores the spiritual principles and practical application of Step Two.

  • Belief Develops Gradually: Step Two doesn't require instant faith—just the willingness to believe that recovery is possible.
  • Letting Go of Old Beliefs: Many addicts come to NA with negative or confused ideas about spirituality. Step Two is about letting go of those that no longer serve us.
  • Restoration is Ongoing: Sanity is restored little by little as we continue in recovery. This includes rational thinking, healthy decisions, and emotional balance.
  • The Role of Experience: By witnessing the recovery of others, we gain evidence that a Power greater than ourselves is at work.
  • Personal Understanding: Each member is encouraged to develop their own concept of a Higher Power, without pressure to conform to any specific belief system.

"It Works" highlights that Step Two is about hope, possibility, and the beginning of a spiritual journey unique to each member.

Insights from the Step Working Guides

The Step Working Guides provide practical questions and exercises for Step Two.

  • What Does "Sanity" Mean to Me?: Encourages reflection on how addiction affected one's thinking and behavior, and what sanity might look like in recovery.
  • Exploring Belief: Prompts members to examine their beliefs about a Higher Power, spirituality, and what it means to trust something outside themselves.
  • Willingness to Change: Asks about openness to new ideas and experiences, and what it would take to believe in the possibility of recovery.
  • Evidence of Restoration: Encourages looking for signs, even small ones, that sanity is being restored—such as better decision-making or less chaos.
  • Barriers to Belief: Helps members identify fears, doubts, or resentments that may block belief in a Power greater than themselves.

The guides help make Step Two a personal, practical process—one of honest self-examination and growing hope.

Key Themes & Synthesis of Step Two

Step Two is the step of hope, possibility, and the beginning of spiritual awakening. Key themes include:

1. Willingness and Open-mindedness

Step Two asks only for willingness to believe in the possibility of change and to be open to new ideas about recovery and spirituality.

2. Hope and Restoration

Hope replaces despair as we begin to believe that sanity and a better life are possible through recovery.

3. The Power of Example

Seeing others recover is powerful evidence that a Power greater than ourselves can work in our lives too.

4. Individual Spiritual Path

Each person's understanding of a Higher Power is personal and unique. Step Two encourages exploration, not conformity.

5. Gradual Process

Belief and restoration to sanity are not instant—they unfold over time as we continue in recovery.

6. Letting Go of the Past

Step Two involves releasing old beliefs and attitudes that kept us trapped in addiction, making room for new growth.

Step Two is the invitation to hope and the first real step toward spiritual awakening and lasting change.
