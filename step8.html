A Deep Dive into Step Eight of Narcotics Anonymous

Step Eight: "Made a list of all persons we had harmed, and became willing to make amends to them all."

Insights from the Basic Text

The Basic Text describes Step Eight as a process of inventory and willingness, preparing for the amends of Step Nine.

  • Making a List: We identify all persons we have harmed, directly or indirectly, through our addiction and related behaviors.
  • Becoming Willing: Willingness is emphasized—being ready to make amends, even if we have reservations or fear about doing so.
  • Honesty and Thoroughness: The list should be as complete and honest as possible, including people we may not want to face.

The Basic Text frames Step Eight as a necessary preparation for the healing process of making amends.

Insights from "It Works: How and Why"

"It Works: How and Why" explores the spiritual and emotional aspects of Step Eight.

  • The Nature of Willingness: Willingness is a spiritual principle that grows as we work the program.
  • Facing Our Past: Making a list of those we have harmed can be challenging, but it is essential for our growth and freedom.
  • Healing Relationships: The process of becoming willing to make amends is a step toward healing relationships and ourselves.

This text highlights that Step Eight is about readiness for change and reconciliation.

Insights from the Step Working Guides

The Step Working Guides provide practical exercises for working Step Eight.

  • Listing Harmed Persons: Guides offer questions to help identify all affected parties, including ourselves.
  • Exploring Willingness: Prompts are given to reflect on what willingness means and how to cultivate it.
  • Preparing for Amends: The guide encourages us to write down our list and to be thorough and honest.

The guides make Step Eight a deeply personal and introspective process.

Key Themes & Synthesis of Step Eight

Step Eight is a process of inventory and willingness, preparing us for the amends of Step Nine. Key themes include:

1. Honesty in Listing

We are called to be honest and thorough in identifying all persons we have harmed, including those we may not want to face.

2. The Importance of Willingness

Willingness is the spiritual foundation of Step Eight, preparing us for the action of amends in Step Nine.

3. Preparation for Healing

Step Eight is a preparation for healing—both for ourselves and for our relationships.

4. Courage and Humility

Making a list and becoming willing requires courage and humility, as we face the consequences of our past actions.

Step Eight is about readiness for change and reconciliation, setting the stage for the healing process of Step Nine.
